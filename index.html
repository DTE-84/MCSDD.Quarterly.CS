The Quarterly Pro Protocol

Automating Missouri DMH Compliance through High-Fidelity Validation and PCSP Data Inheritance.

The Failure Signal

Prior to automation, the MCSDD quarterly reporting process operated on a manual, high-entropy protocol. Support Coordinators faced significant administrative bloat, spending an estimated 45+ minutes per report on redundant data entry from the annual PCSP. This manual workflow introduced three critical "Signal Deviations" that heightened audit risk:

  • Narrative Staleness: Recurrent use of "Static Text" across quarters, a primary trigger for state audit citations.
  • Linguistic Friction: Use of deficit-based terminology ("Refused," "Failed") that contradicts Person-Centered standards.
  • Utilization Gaps: Variance in service delivery exceeding the ±10% audit threshold without mandatory justification.

The Structural Solution

Quarterly Pro was architected as a "Satellite Node" to the PCSP Pro ecosystem. The system leverages PCSP Ingestion to eliminate re-entry and deploys a 5-Point Validation Engine to shield the agency from regulatory oversight errors.

NLP Tone Guard

Scans clinical text for compliance-based language, suggesting high-fidelity alternatives in real-time.

Variance Telemetry

Real-time calculation of unit utilization with automated justification triggers for ±10% deviations.

Discovery Tracking

Dual-timestamp logic ensures MUI (Major Unusual Incident) discovery aligns with the mandatory 24-hour reporting window.

Anti-Similarity Check

A word-overlap algorithm that flags "Rubber Stamping" when narratives exceed an 85% similarity threshold.
